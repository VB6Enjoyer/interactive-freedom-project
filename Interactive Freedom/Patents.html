Are Patents a Hindrance to Progress?

From an Austro-Libertarian perspective, patents are fundamentally not property rights at all. True property rights apply to scarce physical resources – the land you own, the tools you build, the finished goods you produce. These things can only be used by one person at a time, and owning them means having exclusive control over their use.

Ideas, inventions, knowledge – these are non-scarce. My knowing or using a design for a widget doesn't prevent you from also knowing or using that same design. A patent, however, is a grant from the state of a temporary monopoly, preventing others from *using their own physical property* (their factory, their computer, their raw materials) in certain ways if it involves the patented idea. This isn't protecting property; it's restricting action.

Instead of fostering innovation by guaranteeing returns (which free markets do through first-mover advantage, trade secrets, branding, and reputation), patents *stifle* it. They increase costs (licensing fees, legal battles), prevent others from building upon existing knowledge, and divert resources from productive innovation to seeking and defending state privileges.
